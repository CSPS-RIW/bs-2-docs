Introducing the terms

Mental health refers to our state of mental or emotional well-being. Mental illnesses are severe disturbances in thoughts, feelings, and perceptions that affect our day-to-day functioning. As many as 1 in 5 Canadians will experience a mental health problem or illness in a year, while 5 in 5 of us have mental health yet can still experience challenges with our mental health.[1]

Throughout this course, the terms “challenges,” “issues,” and “problems” will be used interchangeably in relation to mental health to refer to any issues that may take someone outside of their normal realm of coping and mental health. These terms are not meant to be scientific labels, but are rather intended to pinpoint any mental health difficulty and possible need for support.

This course does not delve into the distinction between “diagnosed” and “undiagnosed” mental health challenges or their characteristics. Its focus is on discussing mental health in the workplace in general. Similarly, this course does not, nor does it intend to, equip anyone with the tools to diagnose themselves or others. Different mental health realities are highlighted to show a range of experiences and interactions in the workplace and how they may be addressed, not to delve into the particularities of diagnosed versus undiagnosed mental health issues.

[1] Canadian Mental Health Association, Mental health: What is it really?, January 13, 2020.

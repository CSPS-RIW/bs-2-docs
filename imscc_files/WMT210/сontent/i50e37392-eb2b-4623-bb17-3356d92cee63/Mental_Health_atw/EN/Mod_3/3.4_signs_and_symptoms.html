Signs and symptoms of mental health challenges

The signs and symptoms of mental health issues can be very personal. You may wish to use your interoception skills to develop your own list of signs and symptoms that indicate you are outside your mental well-being comfort zone.

To help ground the discussion of this topic, here are some common signs and symptoms that may indicate a person is unwell and would benefit from support, tools, and resources:

Select each item to learn more.

Significant sleep, energy, or appetite changes, or a drop in self-care routines

Depressed feelings, or dramatic and rapid shifts in emotions or a sense of being disconnected from yourself or your surroundings

A loss of initiative or interest in activities previously enjoyed, or social withdrawal

A decline in functioning at work or in your personal life, including quitting activities, having difficulty performing familiar tasks, and failing in educational pursuits

Problems with concentration, logical thought, memory, or speech

Increased sensitivity to sights, smells, touch, and sounds

Illogical or magical thinking typical of childhood, such as unusual or exaggerated views about personal powers to influence events or meanings

Strong negative feelings: anxiety, guilt, irrational fears, suspicion of others

Difficulty understanding and relating to people and situations or uncharacteristic, odd or peculiar behaviour

Suicidal thinking

If you experience any of the listed signs or symptoms, you might find it beneficial to speak with a mental health professional.

The importance of prioritizing mental health in the workplace

Workplaces can support positive mental health by giving people the chance to realize their potential and feel productive. On the other hand, workplaces can also be stressful and psychologically unsafe environments that contribute to an increase in mental health problems and illnesses.[5]

In a nationwide Canadian study published in 2022, 71% of 4,878 online survey respondents indicated that they experienced at least one form of workplace harassment and violence or sexual harassment and violence in the two years prior to their completion of the survey.[6]

Research also shows that currently only 23% of Canadians feel comfortable approaching their employer about their mental illness without fear of discrimination or dismissal.[7] Half a million Canadians miss work weekly because of their mental health. This trend stands to cost the global economy $16 trillion by 2030, which is more than the cost of diabetes, respiratory disorders, and cancer combined.[8]

Supporting employee mental health can improve productivity, cut down on absences, and increase employee retention.

Under the Canada Occupational Health and Safety Regulations, federal public service employers must take all reasonable actions to create and maintain psychologically healthy and safe workplaces. A psychologically healthy and safe workplace is “a workplace that promotes employees’ psychological well-being and actively works to prevent harm to worker psychological health, including in negligent, reckless, or intentional ways.”[9]

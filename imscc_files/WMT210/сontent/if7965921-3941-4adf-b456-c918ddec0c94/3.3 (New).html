Another way to look at it: interoception and the car engine light

We can become aware of the state of our mental health through different signs and symptoms. Recognizing these signs and symptoms can help mitigate additional negative effects and encourage us to seek support sooner. This is an important skill known as “interoception.”

Interoception has to do with 

“the ability to accurately detect and evaluate cues related to physiological reactions.” It involves “the perception of sensations from inside the body and includes the perception of physical sensations related to internal organ function such as heartbeat.”

— [1]

Imagine that our mind is like the engine of a car. While we are driving, we cannot look directly inside the engine. So how do we know whether something is wrong with it? One way is to keep an eye on the engine light on the dashboard. When this light comes on, we know that something could be wrong with the engine. We may not know exactly what the issue is—it might be serious, or not. Perhaps we need to go to a garage, perhaps we can fix the issue ourselves. But we know that continuing to drive with the engine light on is taking a risk.

Just as many people ignore the engine light on their dashboard, many people disregard the signs and symptoms of mental health issues. The first important step is to enhance your skill of interoception and recognize your mental engine light. Writing down your feelings about the events of the day can help exercise this muscle. You may consider keeping a journal.

Coping strategies and support

If you are experiencing signs or symptoms of a mental health issue, there are coping measures you can take to reduce the negative impact. Whenever possible, itâ€™s best to rely on a number of coping strategies in case one method becomes less effective or unavailable. Having a range of formal and informal supports can also be helpful.

Mental health coping strategies require practice. Using these strategies when you feel well can make them more accessible and easier to use when you are feeling unwell. Just as the signs and symptoms of mental health are personal, so too are the coping strategies that support mental well-being.  

Coping strategies and mental health support can include any of the following:

Physical activity and grounding exercises

  • Going for a walk
  • Exercising and participating in sports
  • Doing grounding exercises to deal with panic attacks
  • Engaging in mindfulness

Relaxing and sensory activities

  • Eating nourishing food and snacks
  • Drinking soothing tea
  • Taking a bath
  • Listening to music
  • Getting a massage

Connecting with your circle of family, friends and colleagues

  • Connecting and sharing with trusted family and friends
  • Engaging in peer support through formal or informal groups
  • Reaching out to a mentor

Seeking formal psychological, medical and workplace support

  • Speaking with a psychotherapist, including through the Employee Assistance Program
  • Taking prescribed medication after a discussion with your doctor
  • Reaching out to an Ombud office for an informal discussion about workplace issues

Leveraging community or group membership support

  • Participating in traditional ceremonies
  • Going to a place of worship, like a church or a mosque
  • Meeting and speaking with an Elder
  • Engaging in spiritual practices
  • Reaching out to a group with culturally specific supports (for example, the Federal Black Employee Caucus or a Positive Space group)

The stigma surrounding mental health issues

Stigma generally has to do with the negative perception of something because of a distinguishing mark or characteristic that is considered to be a disadvantage. Negative attitudes towards people who have a mental health condition are common. The stigma is learned through socialization, where stereotypes and a lack of understanding of mental illness prevail.

The stigma surrounding mental illness can lead to direct or indirect discrimination. For example, someone might make a negative comment about your mental illness or treatment, or avoid you, because they assume you could be unstable. The stigma can also be internalized and lead to self-judgment.[18]

The harmful effects of the stigma of mental health issues can include:

  • being reluctant to seek support or treatment for yourself or someone you care about
  • feeling misunderstood by those around you
  • having fewer opportunities for work, higher education, or social activities
  • experiencing harassment, bullying or physical violence
  • believing that you will not succeed at certain challenges

Pause and reflect

What are some ways you can address the stigma of mental health issues in the workplace?

See suggestions

There are several ways you can address the stigma of mental health issues, including:

  • talking openly about mental health
  • educating yourself and others about mental health
  • listening to people share their lived experiences with mental illness
  • using people-first language and not hurtful or derogatory language
  • showing compassion for people who live with a mental illness
  • speaking out against stigma

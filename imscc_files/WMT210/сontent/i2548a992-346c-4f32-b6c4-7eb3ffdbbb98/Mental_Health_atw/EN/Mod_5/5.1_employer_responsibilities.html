Employer responsibilities

Under the Canada Labour Code, Part II, and the Canada Occupational Health and Safety Regulations, employers are responsible for protecting the health and safety of employees at work, which includes protecting them against physical and psychosocial hazards, preventing physical and psychological injuries and illnesses, and preventing workplace harassment and violence. This responsibility also falls on managers for the employees and work areas under their supervision.

Every federal organization must have a hazard prevention program including psychosocial hazards and a workplace harassment and violence prevention policy highlighting the organization’s commitment to a healthy workplace free of harassment and violence. Organizations must also ensure employees have access to information on medical, psychological and other support services available in their geographical area.

The National Standard of Canada for Psychological Health and Safety in the Workplace (CAN/CSA- Z1003) identifies 13 psychosocial factors that affect workplace psychological health and safety. Addressing these factors can positively impact employee mental health and engagement and help prevent harassment and violence.

The Mental Health Commission of Canada has created a guide to help employers implement the standard and build a psychologically healthy and safe workplace (see the relevant resources below). The guide details how employers can:

    • identify and eliminate hazards
    • control hazards that cannot be eliminated
    • implement practices that support and promote psychological health and safety
    • foster a culture that promotes psychological health and safety

The Canada School of Public Service has developed job aids for each of the 13 psychosocial factors, allowing managers to focus on one psychosocial factor at a time. That said, the 13 factors are interrelated, and positive changes in one factor will likely result in positive changes to other factors as well. The Guarding Minds at Work organizational review worksheets and survey tools referenced at the bottom of each job aid help managers to assess the psychosocial factors and improve psychological health and safety in their organization.
